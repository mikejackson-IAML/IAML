A Message from Our President

Dear Colleague,

Today's HR professionals navigate a landscape of continuous regulatory evolution, shifting precedents, and compliance obligations that simply didn't exist when we started IAML more than four decades ago. The pace of change has fundamentally altered what effective training must deliver.

We maintain a simple standard: information that isn't current enough to be defensible in today's workplace challenges doesn't belong in our curriculum. Period.

The professionals who engage with IAML programs recognize something important. In workplace law, outdated information costs far more than the investment required to stay current.

Here's what makes IAML different—and it's a distinction our participants tell us matters tremendously:

Our faculty members aren't just experienced. They're in the trenches right now. They're litigating cases. They're counseling organizations through complex challenges. They're navigating the real ambiguities between regulatory intent and workplace reality.

This means you're learning from practitioners who bring current, practical insight to every session—the kind of up-to-the-minute information you can actually use when you return to your office.

If that level of preparation matters to you and your organization, I would be honored to have IAML contribute to your continued professional development.

Sincerely yours,
